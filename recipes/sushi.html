Sushi Roll Recipe

Home

Making sushi at home can be fun and rewarding. These basic sushi rolls, or makizushi, are made with seasoned rice, nori (seaweed), and a variety of fillings. Whether you’re using fish, vegetables, or just simple ingredients, sushi is an easy and customizable dish to prepare for a meal or snack.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 3 cups water
  • 1/3 cup rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 5 sheets nori (seaweed)
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 4 ounces sushi-grade fish (like tuna or salmon), thinly sliced (optional)
  • Soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger for serving

Steps:

  1. Rinse the sushi rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Combine the rice and water in a pot, then bring to a boil. Reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for 18-20 minutes. Let it sit for 10 minutes off the heat.
  2. Mix rice vinegar, sugar, and salt in a bowl until dissolved. Pour this mixture over the warm rice and gently fold to combine. Allow the rice to cool to room temperature.
  3. Lay a sheet of nori, shiny side down, on a bamboo sushi mat or a clean surface. Spread a thin layer of rice evenly across the nori, leaving about 1 inch at the top.
  4. Add your fillings (cucumber, avocado, fish, etc.) in a row across the center of the rice.
  5. Roll the sushi tightly from the bottom, using the mat or your hands, and seal the edge with a little water.
  6. Slice the roll into 6-8 pieces with a sharp knife, wiping the knife between cuts to keep the slices clean.
  7. Serve with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.
